My Story

How Music Found Me

Music has been a part of my life for as long as I can remember. My parents both studied music in college, and so I was naturally born into the atmosphere. I have played the piano for what feels like my entire life. I now work as a piano teacher and am grateful for the years that brought me to this point.

My love for the cello started many years later when I was in 5th grade. My friend and I were both interested in the idea of the cello and I ended up sticking with it. I played throughout high school and even had the chance to play many times on my mission as well.

Singing is one of those things that just naturally happened in our family. We always saw ourselves as instrumentalists, but our Sundays were spend gathering as a family and singing together in the living room. Since then, we have recorded a couple of albums and filmed a few music videos together.

The Instruments

What I Play

Each instrument has taught me something different about musicality, patience, and self-expression. Below is a quick look at my journey with each one, including the styles and composers I love most.

  1. Piano
    • Started lessons from my mom at a young age
    • Favorite cellist: Yo-Yo Ma
    • Currently teaching beginners at Slope School
    • Favorite genres: Classical, Country, Pop
  2. Cello
    • Began at age 12
    • Favorite pieces: Bach Cello Suite No. 1, Dvorák Concerto
    • Excited to hopefully play with the Symphony Orchestra at BYU in the Fall
  3. Voice / Singing
    • Choir experience with my family growing up
    • Enjoys both acapella and choral singing
    • Styles: Classical, Choral, Contemporary

Teaching

Passing It On

Since returning home from my mission, I've been a piano instructor at Slope School, working with students from beginners to intermediate players. Teaching has deepened my own understanding of technique, theory, and patience.

I love seeing the progress my students make and sharing the joy of music with them. It's rewarding to help them discover their own musical voices and watch their confidence grow.
